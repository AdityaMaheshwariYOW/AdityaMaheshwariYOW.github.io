About Me

A journey from Ottawa to London—pivoting from cello performance to Mathematics & Finance, discovering data science through trading-floor co‑ops, consulting, and impactful government roles. Now freelancing in educational development and financial services, I’m passionate about user-focused data collection and compelling visualizations.

Roots & Pivot

Grew up in Ottawa with annual visits to my grandparents in India—sparking my global curiosity.

Originally set to study cello performance in Manhattan, I pivoted last-minute to Mathematics & Finance. Listen to my Bach suites.

Data Science Discovery

My first co-op as a Data Scientist on a trading floor ignited my passion for data. A Master’s project predicting posture with LSTMs and sensors then led me into consulting.

Government Impact

During COVID, I joined the CRA to support CERB relief—helping administer a $10 M innovation fund to simplify tax filing.

I then built AI at the Privy Council Office to parse the Prime Minister’s public inbox and led the Quality of Life Hub at Statistics Canada to turn social data into insights.

Freelance & Future

Now married and in London, I’m freelancing—developing courses on ocean analytics, collaborating with authors, and driving projects in financial services.

I’m eager to create user-centric data pipelines and craft visualizations that make data-driven stories come alive.
