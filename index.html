After years of tackling quirky data science puzzles, I’ve built this portfolio to showcase my best work and funnel new project opportunities—let’s talk at aditya.yow@gmail.com!

Public Sector

I’ve learned to support social-sector objectives using data: improving engagement on user-facing platforms like the Quality of Life Hub, developing cutting-edge AI for reading the Prime Minister’s public inbox, and wrangling large-scale tax data to identify populations in need of assistance.

Private Sector

I help clients take the next step in their data-driven journey—assessing and prototyping ML solutions (from sentiment pipelines and DGA classifiers to airport-operations optimizations) and shoring up end-to-end pipelines so insights flow seamlessly.

Teaching

I began by designing a 4-week intensive advanced-analytics bootcamp (2 hours/day), then scaled delivery by hiring and managing additional instructors. Now I teach professional development courses at the University of Texas, covering AI, machine learning, and ML engineering.

Projects

My shot at product building—creating a posture-monitoring app with LSTMs and rotation sensors to track trends, applying deep RL to autonomous vehicles for smoother highway traffic, and crafting an AI-powered expense tracker that uses LLMs to auto-categorize transactions.

Research

My research applies data science to policymaking and development economics—using causal forests to extract insights from RCTs and topic modelling to analyze open-text health survey feedback.
